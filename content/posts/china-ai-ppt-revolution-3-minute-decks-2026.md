## The Xiaohongshu Phenomenon: 250 Million Views Can't Be Wrong

The topic "AI做PPT" — literally "AI makes PowerPoint" — accumulated **250 million views** from 890,000 user-generated notes, representing a **+45% week-over-week growth rate**. This places it firmly in the platform's top-10 trending topics, alongside AI thesis writing and AI interview coaching.

What makes this surge remarkable is its composition. Unlike AI coding or model training discussions, which cluster around developers and researchers, the PPT audience is overwhelmingly **non-technical office workers**. Notes with titles like "打工人3分钟用AI做高端PPT教程" (Office worker's 3-minute tutorial for high-end AI PPT) and "领导夸了我一下午的PPT" (My boss praised my PPT all afternoon) dominate the feed. The content is tutorial-heavy, screenshot-rich, and obsessively focused on **speed-to-quality ratio**.

## The Global Toolkit: Four AI Presentation Architectures

The AI presentation market in 2026 is not monolithic. At least four distinct architectural approaches compete for user attention, each with different strengths, pricing models, and geographic footprints. Understanding these architectures is essential for anyone evaluating the space — whether as a user, investor, or competitor.

| Architecture | Representative Tool | Core Mechanism | Speed | Strength | Weakness |
|--------------|-------------------|--------------|-------|----------|----------|
| **AI-Native Generation** | Pi (Presentation Intelligence) | Multi-agent Design Engine creates content + visuals from scratch simultaneously | 10–15 seconds | Unique designs, fastest generation | Limited PowerPoint export fidelity |
| **Card-Based Web** | Gamma | Modular content cards optimized for async sharing and analytics | <60 seconds | Best engagement tracking, web-native | Weak traditional slide export |
| **Template + Smart Slides** | Beautiful.ai | Pre-designed layouts auto-adjust as content is added | Variable | Enterprise brand governance, SOC 2 | Slowest generation, no free plan |
| **Office Suite Integration** | WPS AI, iSlide AI | Deep integration with PowerPoint/Word ecosystem; template libraries + AI fill | 30–120 seconds | Native .pptx output, Chinese templates | Design less "AI-native," more assisted |

The Chinese market is unique in that **domestic tools are winning on integration**, while international tools are winning on innovation. WPS AI and iSlide AI do not attempt to reimagine what a presentation is; they make the existing PowerPoint workflow dramatically faster. Gamma and Pi, by contrast, are trying to replace PowerPoint entirely — an ambition that plays better in Western markets where Google Slides and web-first workflows are already normalized.

## WPS AI: China's Office Software Giant Fights Back

Kingsoft Office — the parent company of WPS Office — holds a commanding position in China's productivity software market. With over **500 million monthly active users**, WPS is the default office suite for Chinese government, enterprise, and education. When Kingsoft launched WPS AI in late 2024, it was not entering a new market; it was upgrading an installed base.

WPS AI's presentation feature operates on a deceptively simple premise: the user types a topic or uploads a document, and the AI generates a complete PowerPoint deck — complete with Chinese-optimized templates, data visualization suggestions, and speaker notes. The entire process takes **under two minutes** for a standard 15-slide deck.

| WPS AI Presentation Feature | Description | Competitive Advantage |
|-----------------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **One-click generation** | Topic → outline → slides → design in 90 seconds | Fastest end-to-end workflow for Chinese users |
| **Document-to-deck** | Upload Word/PDF; AI extracts structure and visualizes | Critical for Chinese enterprises with heavy document workflows |
| **Template library** | 10,000+ templates, including government, academic, and commercial formats | Deeply localized; international tools lack Chinese formal templates |
| **Data visualization** | Auto-generates charts from tabular data in documents | Reduces Excel → PowerPoint friction |
| **Smart polish** | AI adjusts spacing, fonts, colors to match corporate brand kits | Enterprise compliance without design teams |
| **Collaboration** | Real-time multi-user editing within WPS ecosystem | Native to Chinese enterprise IT infrastructure |

The pricing strategy is equally aggressive. WPS AI's premium tier costs **¥198 per year** (approximately $27), a fraction of Microsoft 365 Copilot's $30 per month add-on. For Chinese small businesses and individual users, this price point is transformative — it makes AI-assisted presentation design cheaper than a single lunch in Shanghai.

## iSlide AI: The Template Engine That Knows Chinese Business Culture

If WPS AI is the generalist, iSlide AI is the specialist. iSlide began as a PowerPoint plugin beloved by Chinese designers for its massive template library and color-scheme tools. Its 2025 pivot to AI transformed it into something more powerful: a **culturally-aware presentation engine** that understands the unwritten rules of Chinese business communication.

Chinese business presentations follow different conventions than Western ones. Government reports require specific structural formats. Academic thesis defenses demand particular color palettes (blue for science, red for humanities). Startup pitch decks in China prioritize revenue proof over growth narratives. iSlide AI's training data includes millions of Chinese-language decks, giving it a cultural fluency that international competitors lack.

| iSlide AI Feature | Technical Description | Why It Matters for Chinese Users |
|-------------------|----------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Cultural template matching** | AI selects templates based on industry + occasion (e.g., "State-owned enterprise annual report") | Eliminates design decision fatigue |
| **Color psychology engine** | Recommends palettes based on Chinese business color conventions | Red = prosperity, blue = trust, gold = premium |
| **Font optimization** | Auto-selects between Songti, Heiti, and Microsoft YaHei for readability | Critical for Chinese character density |
| **Layout density control** | Adjusts text-to-white-space ratio based on content type | Chinese presentations often carry more text per slide |
| **Animation conservatism** | Defaults to subtle transitions; flags excessive animation as "unprofessional" | Aligns with Chinese corporate aesthetics |
| **Export fidelity** | Generates native .pptx with 99%+ fidelity to original design | Ensures compatibility with enterprise PowerPoint workflows |

iSlide AI's pricing is even more aggressive than WPS AI: a **free tier with 10 daily generations** and a premium tier at **¥98 per year** (~$13.50). At this price, the tool is essentially free for casual users and nearly free for power users. The business model appears to prioritize user acquisition and data accumulation over immediate revenue — a classic Chinese tech playbook.

The time savings are not theoretical; they are transformative for workers who produce multiple presentations per week.

The consumer adoption pattern in China is sending signals that global enterprise software vendors cannot ignore. Here's what the data reveals about the future of enterprise productivity software.

| Enterprise Insight | Chinese Market Evidence | Global Implication |

## The Road Ahead: From Slide Design to AI Agents

The AI presentation boom in China is best understood not as an isolated trend but as one front in a broader war for **knowledge work automation**. The same users generating PPTs with AI are also using AI for thesis writing, email drafting, data analysis, and meeting summaries. The tools are converging into unified productivity agents.

| Evolution Stage | Timeframe | Capability | Representative Tools |
|-----------------|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Stage 1: Template Assist** | 2023–2024 | AI suggests templates and layouts | Early iSlide, Canva Magic Design |
| **Stage 2: Content Generation** | 2024–2025 | AI writes slide content from prompts | WPS AI v1, Gamma, Beautiful.ai |
| **Stage 3: Full Deck Automation** | 2025–2026 | AI generates complete decks from documents | WPS AI v2, iSlide AI, Pi |
| **Stage 4: Agent Integration** | 2026–2027 | AI agent orchestrates entire meeting workflow | Emerging: pre-meeting brief → deck → post-meeting summary |
| **Stage 5: Predictive Creation** | 2027+ | AI anticipates need and generates deck before user asks | Conceptual: calendar-aware, context-aware agents |

Several Chinese startups are already building toward Stage 4. ByteDance's Coze platform allows users to chain AI agents that read calendar invites, scrape relevant documents, generate presentation drafts, and send them to attendees — all before the human opens their laptop. Alibaba's Tongyi Qianwen is integrating similar capabilities across its enterprise suite. The 3-minute deck of 2026 may become the 30-second deck of 2027, and the zero-touch deck of 2028.

## Conclusion: A Productivity inflection Point

China's AI presentation surge is more than a social media trend. It is a **case study in how AI adoption crosses the chasm from early adopters to mainstream users**. The 250 million Xiaohongshu views, the 890,000 tutorial posts, and the millions of actual WPS AI and iSlide AI users represent a population that has internalized AI as a daily tool — not for coding, not for research, but for the mundane, repetitive, time-consuming work of making slides.

For global technology observers, the lesson is clear: **the markets where AI achieves mass adoption first may not be the ones with the most advanced models, but the ones with the most aggressive pricing and deepest integration into existing workflows**. China's domestic presentation tools are not technologically superior to Gamma or Beautiful.ai; they are structurally superior for their target market. They speak the language, understand the business culture, export to the right formats, and cost less than a coffee.

As Google I/O 2026 approaches and the world anticipates the next wave of AI announcements from Silicon Valley, China's office workers have already voted with their wallets and their lunch breaks. The 3-minute deck is here to stay. The only question is whether international vendors will adapt their pricing, localization, and integration strategies fast enough to compete — or whether they will cede the global mass market to Chinese alternatives that have already figured out what users actually want.
